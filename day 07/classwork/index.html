porsche spyder 918

The Porsche 918 Spyder is a high-performance plug-in hybrid supercar that was produced between 2013 and 2015.
It was Porsche's answer to hybrid hypercars like the McLaren P1 and Ferrari LaFerrari.

Engine and Performance

The Porsche 918 Spyder is powered by a 4.6L naturally aspirated V8 engine combined with two electric motors.
The total output of the powertrain is 887 hp, with 608 hp coming from the V8 and 279 hp from the electric motors.
It produces 944 lb-ft of torque in hybrid mode. The car can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 2.5 seconds and has a top speed of 214 mph (345 km/h). It features a 7-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic transmission for lightning-fast gear shifts.

Hybrid System

The Porsche 918 Spyder is equipped with a 6.8 kWh lithium-ion battery, allowing it to operate as a plug-in hybrid.
It has an electric range of approximately 12 miles (19 km) on a full charge.
The battery can be recharged in about two hours using a 240V charger. In fully electric mode, the car can reach speeds of up to 93 mph (150 km/h), making it both efficient and powerful.

Chassis & Handling

The Porsche 918 Spyder is equipped with an all-wheel drive system that enhances its performance and stability.
It weighs approximately 3,616 lbs (1,642 kg) and features advanced active aerodynamics, including an adaptive rear wing that improves downforce.
The car's chassis is constructed from a carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer monocoque, which provides exceptional strength while keeping the weight to a minimum, contributing to its precise handling and impressive overall performance.
