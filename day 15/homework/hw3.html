Figure skating

Figure skating is a sport where individuals, pairs,
or groups perform choreographed routines on ice,
combining athletic jumps, spins, footwork, and artistic expression.
It is governed by the International Skating Union (ISU)
and is a highlight of the Winter Olympics.

Disciplines in Figure Skating:

Men's Singles - Jumps, spins, and step sequences performed by male skaters.
Women's Singles - Similar to men's but with differences in typical jump combinations.
Pairs Skating - A male and female skater perform together, featuring throws, lifts, and synchronized jumps.
Ice Dance - Focuses on rhythm, footwork, and dance holds (no jumps or throws; lifts are different from pairs).
Synchronized Skating - Teams of 8-16 skaters perform together in unison.

Key Elements:

Jumps

Toe jumps Toe loop, flip, Lutz (skater uses toe pick to launch).
Edge jumps: Salchow, loop, Axel (take off from the edge of the blade).
The Axel is the hardest because it has an extra half-rotation (e.g., a single Axel is 1.5 rotations)

Spins

Upright, sit, camel, and combination spins.
Flying spins (entered from a jump).

Step Sequences

Footwork patterns graded on difficulty and flow.

Lifts & Twists

In pairs skating, lifts and throw jumps are major elements.
